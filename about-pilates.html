About Pilates

A Bit of History

What is Pilates?

Pilates was developed by Joseph Pilates, a German expatriate, who first began to develop his system of exercises while interned as a prisoner of war during World War I. He called his method "Contrology," and after the war it was adopted and popularized by dancers in New York City who valued its ability to build strength without bulk while improving flexibility and control.

Joseph Pilates lived from 1880 to 1967, and the method that bears his name has only grown in popularity in the decades since, evolving with modern exercise science while staying true to his original principles of controlled, mindful movement.

"In 10 sessions you will feel the difference, in 20 sessions you will see the difference, and in 30 you will have a new body."

— Joseph Pilates

The Method We Teach

What is the STOTT PILATES® Method?

STOTT PILATES® was developed by Lindsay G. Merrithew and Moira Merrithew, in collaboration with physical therapists and sports medicine professionals. The method incorporates modern exercise science and principles of spinal rehabilitation and fascial fitness into classical Pilates exercises, making it both highly effective and safe.

STOTT PILATES® is used by rehabilitation clients, professional athletes, and celebrities around the world, and remains one of the most widely respected and scientifically grounded Pilates methods practiced today.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Can anyone do Pilates?+

Yes. Pilates is designed to be customizable, making it accessible and beneficial for people of all fitness levels and ages.

Why is Pilates one of the fastest-growing exercise methods?+

Because it delivers visible, feelable results while being gentle on the joints — and because it can be tailored to virtually any body and any goal, from rehabilitation to elite athletic performance.

How does STOTT PILATES® differ from traditional weight training?+

STOTT PILATES® trains the body through three-dimensional movement in all planes of motion, using spring resistance that mimics the natural muscular contraction of the body. It emphasizes both concentric and eccentric muscle work, is fully customizable to the individual, and works to rebalance the muscles around the joints — improving posture in the process.

Should Pilates replace my regular workout?+

Not necessarily — many clients use Pilates alongside their regular training to improve core strength, posture, and body awareness, which in turn enhances performance in their other activities.

I have a bad back, will I be able to do Pilates?+

Pilates is widely used as an effective tool for post-rehabilitation and back pain relief. Sessions can be customized to work safely around your specific condition.

Is STOTT PILATES® like Yoga?+

While both practices emphasize breath and mind-body connection, STOTT PILATES® is grounded in modern exercise science, spinal rehabilitation principles, and equipment-based spring resistance, distinguishing it from traditional yoga practice.

Can Pilates help me lose weight?+

Pilates builds longer, leaner muscle and improves overall body awareness, which supports a healthier, more active lifestyle as part of a broader wellness routine.

Why do celebrities do Pilates?+

Celebrities including Reese Witherspoon, Ellen DeGeneres, Kate Hudson, Sandra Bullock, and Jennifer Aniston practice Pilates for its ability to build strength, improve posture, and keep the body lean and flexible.

Is it true athletes do Pilates?+

Yes. Athletes such as Kobe Bryant, LeBron James, and Tiger Woods have used Pilates as part of their training, and professional teams including the Orlando Magic, Washington Wizards, Milwaukee Brewers, Detroit Lions, Tampa Bay Buccaneers, and Green Bay Packers have incorporated it into their conditioning programs.

Can I practice Pilates at home?+

Absolutely. Vivian offers online classes via Zoom, allowing you to practice Pilates safely and effectively from the comfort of your own home.
